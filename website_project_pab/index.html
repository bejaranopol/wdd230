"Chimbote" - The Coastal Port that Receives International Merchandise Food

Founded around 1774 in the viceroyalty of Peru, Chimbote is the largest fishing port in the world. Chimbote's active commerce and its diversified industrial development are characteristic of the city. Its population includes workers who have experience in farming, fishing, naval, canning, and the iron-and-steel industry with the company SIDERPERU.
Chimbote also has beaches that support tourism.
